Challah
Price: $22.00 a loaf

Whole loaf of Challah (or sometimes called twisted egg loaf). This Challah was made from an old family recipe gotten from my friend's grandmother-in-law, who owned a Jewish restaurant. I buy the flour from Kansas – from their winter wheat. I tried to make this flour from King Arthur flour, but it never came out the same. It has a wonderful flavor, looks wonderful, browns wonderfully, and tastes fantastic. I am very proud of it. Bagels
Price: $26.00 for a Baker's dozen (13) and $2.75 for one

Fresh large bagels (kind of crispy bottom half) made by a born and bred New York Mom – Crispy bottoms, but soft and "textury" inside. You can actually taste the love.

My bagels – I baked and baked and watched YouTube channels until I had created my own recipe. I wanted the bagels bigger than you can get in the bagel store with a crunchy bottom, that is different from your bagel store. I use more flour and ingredients so I can make the bagels bigger with a special softness inside. And yes, I am an old time native New Yorker and my bagels are dipped in boiling water before they are baked and this makes its consistency that of a perfect New York bagel.

Bialys
Price: $26.00 for a Baker's dozen (13) and $2.75 for one

I pronounce it "Bee-ah-lies". And yes! - They smell good with Roasted Shallots center. Fresh large bialys (kind of crispy bottom half) made by a born and bred New York Mom – Crispy bottoms, but soft and chewy inside. You can actually taste the love. My bialys (unlike my bagels) are not water boiled. They are baked perfectly from a basic Polish recipe.

I looked and tried many different recipes, and came up with one from a combination of YouTube videos and the King Arthur Flour site on the internet. I got it down people! I think you'll love them. Since I have a Cottage permit from the County of Santa Clara, my bagels cannot be made and fried with natural onions (they would be considered "perishable"). However, I have dehydrated shallots or sliced dehydrated onions, which work as well, and form a crispy oniony center on the bialy. I know you'll enjoy them as you spread them with cream cheese.

Almond Thumbprint Cookies
Price: $14.00 for a dozen

I call them black-eyed Susans because of their chocolate center. These are so delicious. The Almond dough when it comes out of the oven and cools has a little salty-almond flavor (very unique). It is made with strictly almond flour which is non-gluten. The ingredients are Almond flour, pure grade A honey, and canola oil and a tiny bit of salt. The topping is a no-sugar-added chocolate chips and water. These 2 ingredients are warmed, blended and piped to make the delectable center. Perfect combination.
